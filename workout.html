Workout Basics

When working out, it is important to focus on what we call progressive overload. Essentially, you want to increase the stress on your muscles over time to promote strength and muscle growth. There is no reason to ego-lift! Start with a lower weight. Ideally, you want to hit 8–12 reps of any exercise. As you get stronger and you can do more than 8–12 reps, increase the weight!

Cardio

Step counts are arbitrary, and unfortunately, not everyone has a smart watch to properly track their steps. At a minimum, you should ideally be walking 30 minutes to an hour a day. If you can't do all of it at once, that’s okay! 10 minutes here, 15 minutes there, as long as you are getting the minimum, it’s perfectly fine.

For ideal fat loss and if you have access to a treadmill, I highly recommend 12–3–30: 12 incline, 3 speed, and 30 minutes.

Upper Body

Upper Body Day 1 (Back & Biceps)

3 sets, 8–12 reps each

  • Pull-ups (use assistance if needed)
  • Lat pulldowns
  • Seated cable row
  • Bicep curls (dumbbell or barbell)
  • Hammer curls or preacher curls

Upper Body Day 2 (Chest & Triceps)

3 sets, 8–12 reps each

  • Chest fly
  • Incline chest press (dumbbell or barbell)
  • Tricep extensions (rope or overhead dumbbell)
  • Dumbbell kickbacks

Legs

Legs Day 1 (Quads & Hamstrings)

3 sets, 8–12 reps each

  • Leg extensions
  • Seated leg curls
  • Squats (Smith machine or free weights)
  • Romanian deadlifts (RDLs) with dumbbells
  • Calf raises

Legs Day 2 (Glutes)

3 sets, 8–12 reps each

  • Hip thrusts (machine or barbell)
  • Cable kickbacks
  • Back extensions with glute bias (lean forward)
  • Abductor machine
  • Adductor machine
