TDEE Calculator

A TDEE calculator, or Total Daily Energy Expenditure calculator, is a tool used to estimate how many calories your body burns in a day. It factors in your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), which represents the calories your body uses at rest, along with the energy you expend through physical activity, exercise, and daily movement. People use a TDEE calculator to determine how many calories they should consume to maintain, lose, or gain weight. It also helps guide fitness planning and provides insight into how calorie intake compares to energy output, allowing for more informed decisions about diet and exercise to support specific health or fitness goals.

Tips for Using Your TDEE

  • Use your TDEE as a baseline. It’s an estimate, not a rule. Adjust based on results every few weeks.
  • If you are just starting out, I recommend setting your activity level to sedentary to accurately calculate your BMR.
  • If your goal is weight loss, aim for a 250–500 kcal deficit daily for steady, sustainable change. If you need to be more aggressive, try a 750 kcal deficit, though this is not recommended for beginners.
  • If you want to build muscle, try a 200–400 kcal surplus to promote lean growth, but only if you have reached your goal weight for fat loss.
  • Recalculate your TDEE when your weight, activity, or goals change significantly.
  • Focus on macronutrient balance. Protein supports recovery and muscle growth, carbs and fats fuel performance.
  • Pair your TDEE plan with movement and mindful eating for long-term results.
